Case Study

Enhancing Learning Through
Interactive Design

Project Management Workbook

A virtual project management course needed a way to bridge the gap between theory and practice. While the course delivered strong foundational content, learners struggled to apply key concepts to real-world scenarios. Engagement levels were inconsistent, and knowledge retention dropped significantly after course completion.

The challenge was to create an interactive workbook that could function both digitally and in print — guiding learners through each phase of the project lifecycle with hands-on exercises, case studies, and reusable templates.

My Approach

I designed a comprehensive project management Workbook to transform passive learning into an active, applied experience.

Key strategies included:

  • Applied learning framework: Integrated exercises and real-world case studies for each project phaseâ€”initiation, planning, execution, monitoring, and closing.
  • Reusable tools and templates: Ready-to-use project charters, risk matrices, Gantt chart templates, and stakeholder maps for practical application beyond the course.
  • Interactive digital experience: Hyperlinked navigation and fillable fields to support seamless digital use, while maintaining print-friendly layouts.
  • Consistent visual design: Clean, professional design system to enhance readability and reinforce key learning points.
The Impact

The workbook elevated the virtual learning experience by providing learners with a structured, hands-on guide they could use during and after the course. Engagement increased as participants actively applied concepts, and post-course surveys showed measurable gains in knowledge retention and confidence in managing projects.

By equipping learners with practical tools, the workbook strengthened the overall effectiveness of the project management training and improved learner satisfaction scores.
